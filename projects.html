Doctoral Research

My doctoral research focused on using solar inertial modes to probe the deep solar interior. By combining observations with theoretical modeling, I investigated how these large-scale oscillations can be used to infer the Sun's internal rotation profile and constrain the degree of convective instability within the convection zone. This work was the first spatially resolved study in inertial mode helioseismology, targeting the rotation rate at high-latitudes in the deep interior.

Research areas: Solar physics · Helioseismology · Inertial modes · Solar rotation · Stellar interiors

Undergraduate Research

During my undergraduate studies, I worked on helioseismology, developing numerical methods to infer the Sun's internal rotation from p-mode frequency splittings. As part of this project, I developed and implemented an RLS inversion code to reconstruct the solar rotation profile from helioseismic observations. This project provided my first experience with inverse problems, numerical methods, and the analysis of large observational datasets, laying the foundation for my later research on solar oscillations. I also continued the work on a second project on helioseismology where I compared the results from two standard codes used for inverting for the solar rotation profile.

Research areas: Helioseismology · Inverse problems · p-mode oscillations · Numerical methods · Solar rotation
